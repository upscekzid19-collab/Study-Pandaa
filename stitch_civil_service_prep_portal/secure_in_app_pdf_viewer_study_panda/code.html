The Great Revolt of 1857

EduMastery Premium Notes Class: UPSC/Civil Services

Introduction

The Revolt of 1857, often termed as the "First War of Independence," was a watershed moment in colonial Indian history. It began as a mutiny of sepoys in Meerut and quickly spread across North and Central India, shaking the very foundations of the British East India Company's rule.

flash_on IMMEDIATE CAUSE

The introduction of the Enfield Rifle and the rumor that its cartridges were greased with beef and pork fat offended both Hindu and Muslim sepoys.

Nature of the Revolt

Political: The Doctrine of Lapse implemented by Lord Dalhousie caused widespread resentment among Indian princely states.

Socio-Religious: Interference in social customs like Sati and introduction of Western education were viewed as attacks on tradition.

Economic: Heavy taxation and the destruction of traditional handicraft industries led to extreme poverty and peasant uprisings.
